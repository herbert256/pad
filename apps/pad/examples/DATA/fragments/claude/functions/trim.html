The trim function removes whitespace from the beginning and end of a string.

Basic Usage

{echo $spacedText | trim}

Result: lots of spaces

Leading/Trailing Spaces

{echo '  hello  ' | trim}

Trimmed: [hello]

Newlines and Tabs

{echo '
  text
' | trim}

Clean: text

Chained Examples

{echo $spacedText | trim | upper}

Trimmed and uppercase: LOTS OF SPACES

{echo '  john smith  ' | trim | capitalize}

Clean name: John Smith

{echo $email | trim | lower}

Normalized email: john.smith@example.com

Note

Trim only removes whitespace from edges, not from within the string. Use white to normalize internal spaces.
