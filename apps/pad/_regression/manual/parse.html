PAD parses on the fly, on demand, it searches for the first }, then it takes the first { before it, and analyses it to see if it is a PAD tag of PAD variable.

In below example, the {$myVar} part is parsed first and executed, lets assume {$myVar} has the value ABC. 

{pad myOption='{$myVar}'}
   ....
{/pad}

The result is that then below is parsed, and now it will be the {pad} tag that is executed. 

{pad myOption='ABC'}
   ....
{/pad}

If you do not put quotes around {$myVar} then below will be executed, and PAD will give the error that the constant ABC does not exists 

{pad myOption=ABC}
   ....
{/pad}
