The replace function replaces all occurrences of a substring.

Basic Usage

{echo 'hello world' | replace('world', 'there')}

Result: hello there

Remove Characters

{echo 'a-b-c-d' | replace('-', '')}

No dashes: abcd

Change Separators

{echo 'one_two_three' | replace('_', ' ')}

Spaces: one two three

Chained Examples

{echo $email | before('@') | replace('.', ' ') | capitalize}

Name from email: John Smith

{echo 'Hello World' | lower | replace(' ', '-')}

Slug: hello-world

{echo 'a.b.c' | replace('.', '/') | replace('a', 'x')}

Multiple replacements: x/b/c

Note

Replaces ALL occurrences. For removing text, you can also use cut.
