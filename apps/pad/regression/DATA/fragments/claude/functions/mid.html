The mid function returns a substring starting at position (1-based index).

Basic Usage 

{'Hello World' | mid(7, 5)}

From pos 7, length 5: 'Hello World' | mid(7, 5) 

{'Hello World' | mid(1, 5)}

From pos 1, length 5: 'Hello World' | mid(1, 5)

Extract Date Parts 

{'2024-12-14' | mid(1, 4)}

Year: '2024-12-14' | mid(1, 4) 

{'2024-12-14' | mid(6, 2)}

Month: '2024-12-14' | mid(6, 2) 

{'2024-12-14' | mid(9, 2)}

Day: '2024-12-14' | mid(9, 2)

Chained Examples 

{'ISBN-978-3-16-148410-0' | mid(6, 3) | + 0}

ISBN prefix: 'ISBN-978-3-16-148410-0' | mid(6, 3) | + 0

Complex Chain 

{'2024-12-14' | mid(6, 2) | + 0} / {'2024-12-14' | mid(9, 2) | + 0} / {'2024-12-14' | mid(1, 4)}

Reformat date: '2024-12-14' | mid(6, 2) | + 0 / '2024-12-14' | mid(9, 2) | + 0 / '2024-12-14' | mid(1, 4)
